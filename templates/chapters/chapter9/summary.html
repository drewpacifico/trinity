Chapter 9

Summary Sheet

Why Follow-Up Matters

  • 80% of sales occur between 5th-12th contact (yet 44% of salespeople give up after one attempt)
  • Timing is everything: Prospects may be interested but circumstances aren't right yet
  • Familiarity builds trust: Repeated contact increases recognition and confidence
  • Persistence demonstrates work ethic and commitment
  • Most competitors give up quickly: Your persistence is competitive advantage

Initial Follow-Up Sequence (First 90 Days)

  • Day 1: Thank-you email with summary and value content (same day)
  • Day 3-4: Brief value email (article, market update, tip)
  • Day 7-10: Phone call checking in, asking about materials sent
  • Day 14-21: Another value email (different content)
  • Day 30: Phone call, brief check-in
  • Day 45: Email with specific trial offer or low-risk proposal
  • Day 60: Phone call asking what needs to happen before they can move forward
  • Day 90+: Move to monthly nurture cycle

Long-Term Nurture (After 90 Days)

  • Monthly touchpoints alternating email and phone
  • Content types: Market updates, industry news, seasonal planning, education, success stories, tips
  • Quarterly deeper engagement: In-person visits, freight analysis, lunch/coffee, personalized gestures
  • Annual comprehensive outreach: Detailed market analysis, rate benchmarking, complimentary consultation, planning guide

Quote Follow-Up Sequence

  • 2-4 hours: Confirmation email with written details
  • Next day: Brief call/email confirming receipt and checking questions
  • 48 hours: Call to verify received and if modifications needed
  • One week: Check if timeline changed or evaluating multiple options
  • If lost: Thank professionally, request feedback, position as backup, maintain quarterly contact

Technology Tools

  • CRM essential features: Contact management, activity tracking, task management, pipeline visualization, reporting, email integration, mobile access
  • CRM options: HubSpot (free), Zoho, Freshsales (entry), Salesforce, Pipedrive (mid-tier), industry TMS
  • Setup: Import complete data, define sales stages, create templates, automate reminders, daily review habit
  • Email automation: Acceptable for thank-yous, confirmations, scheduled content; avoid obvious templates and excessive frequency

Key Metrics to Track

  • Contact-to-conversation: 15-25% target (attempts needed to reach prospect)
  • Conversation-to-quote: 40-60% target (conversations resulting in quotes)
  • Quote-to-close: 20-35% new customers, 50-70% existing (quotes converting to bookings)
  • Follow-up-to-response: 25-40% target (follow-ups generating responses)
  • Average touchpoints to close: 5-15 typical range
  • Time-to-first-load: 30-120 days typical

Daily Workflow

  • Morning (30-60 min): Review CRMCRM (Customer Relationship Management)A database tracking all prospect and customer information, interactions, and scheduled activities. It's the foundation of systematic follow-up. tasks, prioritize, prepare, make calls (9-11 AM)
  • Midday (30 min): Follow-up emails, value content, CRM updates
  • Afternoon (30-60 min): More calls (2-4 PM), respond to inquiries, end-of-day logging
  • Total: 90-150 minutes daily dedicated to follow-up
  • Batch similar activities, use templates wisely, schedule everything, protect time, work ahead

When to Stop Following Up

  • Direct request to stop (respect immediately)
  • Multiple no-shows without rescheduling
  • 8-10 attempts over 90 days with zero response
  • Business closed or fundamentally changed
  • Bad fit revealed through conversations
  • Decision matrix after 90 days: High potential (monthly forever), medium (quarterly), low (annual or remove)
  • Send professional breakup email before stopping

Customer Follow-Up

  • Per shipment: Before (confirm details), during (pickup confirmation, in-transit updates, alert to issues), after (delivery confirmation, feedback, PODProof of Delivery (POD)Signed documentation confirming freight was delivered, required for invoicing customers and proving shipment completion.)
  • Weekly: Tier A high-volume customers (review recent, discuss upcoming)
  • Monthly: Tier B moderate-volume (overall performance review)
  • Quarterly: All significant customers (performance review, planning, improvements, relationship)
  • Annual: Major customers (deep-dive analysis, strategic planning, health assessment, value demonstration)
  • Service recovery: Immediate (frequent updates), 24 hours (satisfaction confirmation), one week (written summary), one month (check lingering concerns)
