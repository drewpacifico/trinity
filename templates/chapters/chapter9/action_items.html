Chapter 9

Action Items

Complete these tasks to build an effective follow-up system:

  • Set up or optimize your CRMCRM (Customer Relationship Management)A database tracking all prospect and customer information, interactions, and scheduled activities. It's the foundation of systematic follow-up. system: Choose appropriate CRM for your needs, import all contacts with complete information, configure for your sales process
  • Create follow-up email templates (5-10 common scenarios): Day 1 thank-you, Day 3-4 value content, quote confirmation, monthly check-in, quarterly review invitation, value-add content sharing, breakup email
  • Develop 90-day follow-up sequence for new prospects: Document specific actions and timing for Days 1, 3-4, 7-10, 14-21, 30, 45, 60, 90 with exact email text and call scripts
  • Create monthly nurture content calendar: Plan 12 months of value-add content: market updates, industry news, seasonal planning guides, educational tips, case studies
  • Establish daily follow-up time blocks in calendar: Schedule morning (9-11 AM) and afternoon (2-4 PM) blocks, protect from interruptions, create daily CRM review routine
  • Build decision matrix for evaluating long-term prospects: Create criteria for high/medium/low potential classification, document when to move prospects between categories
  • Design quarterly customer review template: Structure for performance metrics, upcoming planning, improvement opportunities, relationship strengthening
  • Set up tracking spreadsheet for follow-up metrics: Weekly tracking of contact-to-conversation, conversation-to-quote, quote-to-close, follow-up-to-response ratios
  • Write your "final breakup email" template: Professional, gracious language for prospects after 8-10 non-responses, maintaining open door for future
  • Schedule weekly time to review and adjust follow-up effectiveness: Friday afternoon analytics review: what worked, what didn't, conversion rates, adjustments needed
  • Create customer communication schedule: Document when to contact Tier A (weekly), Tier B (monthly), Tier C (quarterly), set recurring calendar reminders
  • Develop service recovery follow-up protocol: Template and timeline for immediate response, 24-hour check-in, one-week summary, one-month follow-up
