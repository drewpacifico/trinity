Module 9.3

Long-Term Nurture Strategy

For prospects not yet ready to buy, maintain presence without being pushy through a long-term nurture campaign. The goal is staying visible so when their situation changes, you're top-of-mind.

Monthly Touchpoints

Alternate between email and phone contact to vary your approach and respect their time.

  • Month 1: Email with valuable content
  • Month 2: Brief check-in phone call
  • Month 3: Email with market update or relevant article
  • Month 4: Phone call
  • Month 5: Email
  • And so on...

Content Ideas for Nurture Emails

Market Updates

  • "Peak shipping season update: Here's what we're seeing in your primary lanes and how it might affect your operations..."
  • "Q4 capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates. outlook: Based on current trends and historical patterns, here's what to expect for freight availability and pricing heading into your busy season..."

  • "New regulations affecting [their industry] that you should know about: [Summary of regulatory change and how it impacts their freight]..."

Quarterly Deeper Engagement

Every three months, consider higher-value touchpoints:

  • In-person visit: If geographically practical, offer to stop by for a brief meeting
  • Freight analysis: Offer to review their shipping patterns and provide optimization recommendations
  • Lunch or coffee: Informal relationship-building outside the office
  • Personalized gift: Appropriate branded item or thoughtful gesture during holidays

Annual Comprehensive Outreach

Once yearly, provide substantial value even to long-term nurture prospects:

  • Detailed market analysis: Comprehensive report on their industry's freight trends
  • Rate benchmarking: Analysis of how their rates compare to market averages
  • Complimentary consultation: Offer to review their logistics operation and provide recommendations
  • Planning guide: Customized capacity and rate planning document for the upcoming year
★ Key Takeaway
Long-term nurture is an investment in future revenue. Many of your best customers started as prospects who weren't ready for months or even years. Consistent, value-focused contact ensures you're their first call when circumstances change.

Practical application

Content types for nurture emails:

  • Market updates: Capacity trends, rate changes, seasonal outlooks for their lanes.
  • Industry news: Regulatory changes, economic indicators, competitive landscape.
  • Educational content: Tips on detention, classification, carrier vetting, best practices.
  • Success stories: How you helped similar customers solve problems.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Monthly contact minimum: Alternate between email and phone each month.
  • Value over sales: Nurture content should help, not pitch.
  • Play the long game: Many of your best customers started as prospects who weren't ready for months.
