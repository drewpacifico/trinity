Chapter 6

Action Items

Complete these tasks to master regulations and restrictions:

  • Memorize basic HOSHours of Service (HOS)Federal regulations limiting driver operating hours: 11-hour driving limit, 14-hour duty window, 60/70-hour work week, 30-minute break requirement. limits: 11 hours driving, 14 hours duty, 60/70 work week, 30-min break. Practice calculating realistic transit times for various distances.
  • Understand weight distribution requirements: Study how weight must distribute across axles, not just total gross weight. Practice identifying potential weight distribution problems.
  • Learn when hazmatHazmat (Hazardous Materials)Regulated substances posing risks to health, safety, property, or environment, classified into nine DOT classes and requiring special handling. endorsements required: Memorize the 9 DOTDOT (Department of Transportation)The federal parent agency overseeing transportation policy, including FMCSA and other modal administrations. hazmat classes and recognize common examples (paint, aerosols, chemicals, batteries). Understand placarding thresholds.
  • Research FSMA requirements for food products: Study temperature control, pre-coolingPre-CoolingBringing a refrigerated trailer to required temperature before loading begins, essential since reefer units maintain temperature but don't rapidly cool warm freight., cleaning, and documentation requirements. Understand which foods are most regulated.
  • Study carrierCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. insurance requirements: Know minimums ($750K general, $5M hazmat), understand cargo insurance vs. liability insurance, learn when higher coverage needed.
  • Create checklist for special delivery requirements: Build reference for liftgateLiftgateHydraulic platform attached to trailers that raises and lowers freight at locations lacking loading docks, with 3,000-5,000 pound capacity limits., residential, limited access, inside delivery, appointments, security procedures with typical costs.
  • Understand when permits needed: Memorize legal dimensions (8.5' wide, 13.5-14' tall, 53' long, 80K lbs). Know process and lead times for oversizeOversize/Overweight (OS/OW)Freight exceeding legal dimensions (8.5' wide, 13.5-14' tall, 53' long) or weight (80,000 lbs), requiring special permits and possibly escorts./overweight permits.
  • Build detentionDetentionTime drivers spend waiting beyond agreed 'free time' (typically 2 hours) at shipping or receiving facilities, usually charged at $25-75 per hour. communication strategy: Develop language explaining free time, detention charges, and HOS impact to customers during load booking.
  • Study cross-border basics: If serving international customers, learn documentation requirements, authority needs, typical border crossing times in your region.
  • Create regulatory quick reference guide: One-page summary of most important limits and requirements you'll reference frequently.
  • Practice explaining regulations to customers: Role-play explaining why certain requests violate HOS rules or why specific requirements exist. Build confidence in regulatory knowledge.
  • Verify carrier vetting procedures with brokerage: Confirm your brokerage's process for checking authority, insurance, safety ratings. Understand your responsibilities.
