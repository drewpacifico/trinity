Chapter 5

Action Items

Complete these tasks to master load types and cargo categories:

  • Create freight type reference guide: Build a one-page chart showing major freight categories, their characteristics, equipment needs, and special requirements
  • Learn temperature requirements for common food products: Memorize frozen (0°F to -10°F), dairy (34-38°F), fresh meat (28-32°F), and understand produce varies widely by product
  • Understand when hazmatHazmat (Hazardous Materials)Regulated substances posing risks to health, safety, property, or environment, classified into nine DOT classes and requiring special handling. endorsements required: Study the 9 DOTDOT (Department of Transportation)The federal parent agency overseeing transportation policy, including FMCSA and other modal administrations. hazmat classes and recognize common examples (paint, aerosols, chemicals, batteries)
  • Study LTLLess-Than-Truckload (LTL)Freight service consolidating shipments from multiple shippers (typically 150-10,000 pounds), priced by freight class based on density, stowability, handling, and liability. classification basics: Learn how density, stowability, handling, and liability affect freight class and pricing
  • Research specialized equipment availability in your market: Identify carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. in your area who handle flatbedFlatbed TrailerAn open platform trailer (48' or 53' long) without sides or roof, used for freight requiring top/side loading or exceeding enclosed trailer dimensions., reeferRefrigerated Trailer (Reefer)Insulated trailers with refrigeration units maintaining temperatures from -20°F to +70°F. Essential for produce, dairy, meat, and temperature-sensitive goods., heavy haul, hazmat—build carrier database by specialty
  • Develop questions to ask customers about freight characteristics: Create checklist: weight (including pallets?), dimensions, stackable?, temperature requirements?, previous load restrictions?, special handling?, hazmat?, delivery equipment available?
  • Learn common accessorialAccessorial ChargesAdditional fees charged by carriers for services beyond standard dock-to-dock delivery, such as liftgate service, inside delivery, residential delivery, or appointment scheduling. charges: Know typical costs for liftgateLiftgateHydraulic platform attached to trailers that raises and lowers freight at locations lacking loading docks, with 3,000-5,000 pound capacity limits. ($50-150), inside delivery ($75-200), residential ($75-150), limited access ($50-100)
  • Study food safety requirements: Research FSMA basics, food-grade trailer requirements, common previous load restrictions
  • Understand high-value freight protocols: Learn security requirements: team driversTeam DriversTwo qualified drivers alternating driving and resting, allowing near-continuous movement covering 1,000-1,200+ miles in 24 hours at 50-75% premium rates., GPS tracking, secure parking, sealed trailers
  • Practice identifying freight category from customer descriptions: Take sample loads and determine: equipment type, special requirements, potential challenges, appropriate rate premium
  • Build commodity-specific carrier relationships: Identify 3-5 carriers who specialize in each major category you plan to handle (reefer specialists, flatbed specialists, hazmat carriers, etc.)
  • Create rate adjustment guidelines: Document typical premiums: reefer (+20-40%), hazmat (+15-30%), team drivers (+50-75%), high-value (+insurance costs), expeditedExpedited FreightShipments requiring faster-than-normal transit, often using team drivers to cover 1,000-1,500 miles in 24 hours.
