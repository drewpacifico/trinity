High-Value Cargo

High-value cargo typically means freight worth $100,000 or more, though some customers consider anything above $50,000 high-value. This freight requires additional security measures and insurance.

⚠ Important
Standard cargo insurance only covers up to $100,000 per load. High-value freight requires additional insurance purchased for the specific shipment, typically costing 1-3% of declared value. Confirm your brokerage's insurance capabilities before accepting high-value loads.

Common High-Value Freight Types

  • Consumer electronics: Televisions, computers, smartphones, tablets, gaming systems. Electronics are both valuable and attractive to thieves.
  • Telecommunications equipment: Network equipment, servers, switches, routers. Both valuable and easy to resell.
  • Medical electronics and equipment: Diagnostic equipment, surgical instruments, hospital electronics. Extremely expensive and specialized.
  • Prescription drugs: Certain pharmaceutical products, especially high-cost specialty medications. Can be worth hundreds of thousands or millions per load.
  • Jewelry and precious metals: Rare in truckload freight but occasionally moved. Requires extensive security.
  • Designer goods and luxury products: High-end fashion, luxury goods, premium products with strong resale markets.
  • Computer components and semiconductors: Processors, memory chips, graphics cards. Small, valuable, and easily stolen.

Security Measures for High-Value Freight

High-value freight requires security protocols beyond standard shipments:

Team Drivers

  • Two qualified drivers in the truck at all times
  • One driver can rest while the other drives
  • Continuous movement reduces theft opportunities
  • Truck is never left unattended

GPS Tracking

  • Real-time location monitoring
  • Frequent position updates (every 15-30 minutes)
  • Geofencing alerts if truck deviates from planned route
  • Immediate notification of unexpected stops

Secure Parking

  • Approved facilities only (no truck stops or rest areas)
  • Monitored locations with security
  • Well-lit areas with surveillance
  • Fenced and gated compounds preferred

Sealed Trailers

  • Tamper-evident seals applied at origin
  • Seal numbers recorded and verified
  • No unauthorized access during transit
  • Broken seals immediately reported

Carrier Vetting for High-Value Freight

Not every carrier can handle high-value freight. Vetting requirements typically include:

  • Minimum years in business (often 3+ years)
  • Clean safety record
  • Proven experience with high-value freight
  • GPS tracking capabilities
  • Written security protocols
  • Driver background check programs

Practical application

Questions to ask when high-value freight is involved:

  • "What is the declared value of this shipment?" Determines insurance requirements.
  • "Do you require team drivers?" Continuous movement reduces theft risk.
  • "What security protocols do you expect?" GPS tracking, secure parking, sealed trailers.
  • "Do you have specific carrier requirements?" Many high-value shippersShipperA business needing to move goods from one location to another, including manufacturers, distributors, retailers, and other customer types. have approved carrier lists.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Insurance awareness: Standard cargo insurance covers $100,000. High-value loads need additional coverage.
  • Security requirements: Team drivers, GPS tracking, and secure parking are common requirements.
  • Carrier vetting: Only use established carriers with proven security track records for high-value freight.

High-value freight commands premium rates (50-75% more for team drivers) but requires careful carrier selection and enhanced monitoring.
