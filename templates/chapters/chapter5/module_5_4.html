Building Materials and Construction Products

Construction materials move in large volumes and often require specialized equipment and handling. Understanding common construction freight types helps you serve this market effectively.

Lumber and Dimensional Wood Products

Lumber is one of the most common construction materials moving by truck. It ships primarily on flatbedsFlatbed TrailerAn open platform trailer (48' or 53' long) without sides or roof, used for freight requiring top/side loading or exceeding enclosed trailer dimensions. due to length and loading requirements.

Key characteristics:

  • Usually ships in bundles (multiple pieces banded together)
  • Lengths vary from 8 feet to 20+ feet
  • Requires proper securing with straps (chains can damage wood)
  • Almost always requires tarpingTarpingCovering flatbed freight with large protective tarps (typically 18×24 feet) to protect from weather, debris, or road spray, adding $50-150 to costs. (moisture damages lumber and creates liability)
  • Relatively heavy (loads typically weigh out)

When quoting lumber loads, confirm:

  • Exact lengths (affects trailer requirements and permits)
  • Bundle sizes and weights
  • Tarping requirements (customer or carrierCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. provides tarps?)
  • Loading method (usually crane or forklift from sides)

Lumber rates fluctuate significantly with construction activity and raw material prices. During housing booms, lumber freight becomes highly competitive.

Drywall (Gypsum Board)

Drywall ships on flatbeds in large quantities. It's heavy, fragile, and extremely moisture-sensitive.

Key characteristics:

  • Very heavy for its size (loads weigh out quickly)
  • Extremely fragile (requires careful securing to prevent breakage)
  • Moisture destroys it (tarping absolutely required)
  • Usually ships in large sheets (4x8 feet, 4x10 feet, 4x12 feet)
  • Requires careful handling (broken drywall is worthless)
⚠ Important
Tarping is absolutely critical for drywall—even brief rain exposure can ruin entire loads worth thousands of dollars. Never transport drywall without tarping, regardless of weather forecasts. Moisture destroys it completely.

Drywall carriers often specialize in this freight because proper securing techniques prevent damage. Random flatbed carriers without drywall experience may break sheets through improper securing.

Steel Products

Steel moves in various forms, each with unique characteristics:

  • Steel beams and structural steel: Long, heavy, typically flatbed. Weather-resistant but may require securing with chains.
  • Steel plate: Flat sheets of varying thickness. Very heavy, requires proper load distribution.
  • Steel coils: Rolled steel for manufacturing. Extremely heavy and dangerous if improperly secured. Requires specialized coil racks or cradles.
  • Rebar: Reinforcing bars for concrete. Bundles of long, heavy steel bars.
⚠ Common Mistake
Steel coils are extremely dangerous if improperly secured. A 40,000-pound coil shifting during transit can cause catastrophic accidents. Only experienced carriers with proper coil equipment should handle this freight.

Windows and Glass

Glass products require specialized handling and equipment:

  • Extremely fragile (requires careful securing and handling)
  • Often ships in specialized racks or crating
  • May require air-ride suspension trailers
  • Premium rates due to fragility and specialized handling

Practical application

Questions to ask when booking building materials:

  • "What are the exact lengths?" Lumber over 48 feet may need permits or specialized equipment.
  • "Does this require tarping?" Almost all lumber and drywall must be tarped.
  • "How will this be loaded?" Confirm crane or forklift availability.
  • "Is this going to a construction site?" Site access may be limited or muddy.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Tarping requirements: Moisture destroys lumber and drywall. Always confirm tarping is included.
  • Weight awareness: Building materials are heavy. Steel and drywall loads typically weigh out, not cube out.
  • Flatbed basics: Most construction materials ship on flatbeds. Know the equipment requirements.

Construction freight follows economic cycles. During building booms, this segment becomes highly competitive and profitable.
