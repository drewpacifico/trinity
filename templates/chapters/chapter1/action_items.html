Chapter 1

Action Items

Complete these tasks to build your foundation and accelerate your learning:

  • Write your value proposition: In your own words, explain why a business would choose to work with you instead of calling carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. directly. Practice saying this out loud until it feels natural
  • Confirm your commission structure: Review your specific commission split, payment terms, and any fees or requirements with your brokerage manager
  • Create a personal financial plan: Map out how you'll cover expenses during the 3-6 month ramp-up period while building your customer base
  • Subscribe to industry news: Sign up for FreightWaves, Transport Topics, or DATDATA major load board (online marketplace) connecting available loads with available trucks, also providing market rate intelligence and capacity data. Trendlines to start learning how industry professionals talk about the market
  • Listen to freight podcasts: Find 2-3 trucking or logistics podcasts and listen during your commute or downtime. Recommendations: FreightWaves' WHAT THE TRUCK?!?, Truck Parking Podcast, or The Loadboard
  • Follow industry voices on LinkedIn: Connect with freight brokersBrokerAn individual or company licensed by the FMCSA to arrange transportation of freight between shippers and carriers without owning trucks. Brokers find capacity, negotiate rates, and coordinate shipments, earning revenue from the margin between what shippers pay and what carriers receive., carriers, and logistics professionals. Pay attention to how they discuss challenges and solutions
  • Learn the language: Start a personal glossary of terms you hear that you don't understand. Look them up and write definitions in your own words
  • Study a map: Familiarize yourself with major freight corridors, key cities, and geographic regions. Understanding where freight flows helps you think like a broker
