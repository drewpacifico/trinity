Chapter 1

Action Items

Complete these tasks to build your foundation and accelerate your learning:

  • Write your value proposition: In your own words, explain why a business would choose to work with you instead of calling carriers directly. Practice saying this out loud until it feels natural
  • Confirm your commission structure: Review your specific commission split, payment terms, and any fees or requirements with your brokerage manager
  • Create a personal financial plan: Map out how you'll cover expenses during the 3-6 month ramp-up period while building your customer base
  • Subscribe to industry news: Sign up for FreightWaves, Transport Topics, or DAT Trendlines to start learning how industry professionals talk about the market
  • Listen to freight podcasts: Find 2-3 trucking or logistics podcasts and listen during your commute or downtime. Recommendations: FreightWaves' WHAT THE TRUCK?!?, Truck Parking Podcast, or The Loadboard
  • Follow industry voices on LinkedIn: Connect with freight brokers, carriers, and logistics professionals. Pay attention to how they discuss challenges and solutions
  • Learn the language: Start a personal glossary of terms you hear that you don't understand. Look them up and write definitions in your own words
  • Study a map: Familiarize yourself with major freight corridors, key cities, and geographic regions. Understanding where freight flows helps you think like a broker
