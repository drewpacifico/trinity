The Agent Business Model

As a freight agent, you work as a salaried employee with commission incentives for a licensed freight brokerage. Your income consists of a base salary that provides financial stability, plus commission earnings based on the gross margin you generate from the shipments you arrange.

When you arrange a shipment, you quote a rate to your customer. You then find a carrier willing to haul that load for less. The difference between what the customer pays and what the carrier receives is the gross margin. Your brokerage pays you a base salary for your work, and you earn additional commission based on the margins you generate.

💰 Example
Customer pays $1,500, carrier receives $1,100, gross margin is $400. You receive your regular salary plus a commission percentage on that $400 margin, creating additional earning potential beyond your base compensation.

This Compensation Structure Provides Several Advantages

Income Stability

Your base salary ensures consistent income while you develop your book of business, removing the financial stress that purely commission-based roles create.

Performance Rewards

Commission earnings reward your effectiveness at building customer relationships, negotiating favorable rates, and managing profitable operations. The more successful you become, the more you earn.

Alignment of Interests

Your compensation aligns with your brokerage's success. When you generate healthy margins through excellent service and strong relationships, everyone benefits.

Growth Potential

As your customer base expands and your skills improve, your commission earnings grow substantially. Successful agents significantly increase their total compensation over time.
