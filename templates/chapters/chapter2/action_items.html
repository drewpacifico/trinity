Action Items

Complete these tasks to apply your understanding of the industry landscape:

  • Identify 3-5 shipperShipperA business needing to move goods from one location to another, including manufacturers, distributors, retailers, and other customer types. types to target: Based on your local market, interests, and background, determine which customer types offer the best opportunities for you
  • Research major carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. in your region: Use FMCSAFMCSA (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration)The primary regulatory body for trucking, issuing operating authority, enforcing safety regulations, and maintaining carrier safety ratings.'s database to identify asset-based carriers operating in your area. Note their equipment types and service areas
  • Study current capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates. conditions: Check load boardsLoad BoardOnline marketplace connecting available loads with available trucks (DAT, Truckstop.com, 123Loadboard), also providing market rate intelligence., read industry publications, and talk to carriers to understand whether capacity is currently tight or loose in your primary markets

  • Subscribe to industry publications: Sign up for FreightWaves, Transport Topics, or other industry news sources to stay informed about market conditions and trends
  • Map your competitive landscape: List the brokerages, 3PLs3PL (Third-Party Logistics Provider)A company that provides comprehensive logistics services beyond just transportation brokerage, including warehousing, inventory management, order fulfillment, and freight forwarding., and other competitors serving your target market.
