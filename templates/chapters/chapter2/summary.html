Chapter 2

Summary: Understanding the Industry Landscape

  • The Freight Ecosystem Participants:
  • Shippers (your customers): Manufacturers, distributors, retailers, construction companies, agricultural businesses, e-commerce companies, each with unique needs and challenges
  • Carriers (your service providers): Owner-operators, small/medium/large fleets, asset-based and non-asset carriers with different capabilities and service levels
  • Intermediaries: Freight brokerages, 3PLs, freight forwarders, load boards connecting various ecosystem participants
  • Regulators: FMCSA (primary), DOT (parent agency), state agencies, industry associations setting standards and requirements
  • Money Flow: Customer pays you → You pay carrier → Margin is split based on commission agreement (typically 50-70% to agent)
  • Market Dynamics:
  • Capacity: Tight (more loads than trucks = higher rates) vs. Loose (more trucks than loads = lower rates)
  • Seasonality: Pre-holidays, harvest seasons, month/quarter-end create tight capacity; winter typically slower
  • Other factors: Economic conditions, weather events, regulatory changes, fuel prices affect rates
  • Competition: Other agents, large brokerages, asset-based carriers with brokerage arms, 3PLs, digital platforms, direct carrier relationships, each with distinct advantages and disadvantages
  • Specialization Options: Geographic (lanes, regions, rural), industry (food, construction, manufacturing), equipment (flatbed, reefer, heavy haul), service level (expedited, white-glove, cross-border)
