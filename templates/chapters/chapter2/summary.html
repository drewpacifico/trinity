Chapter 2

Summary Sheet

The Freight Ecosystem

  • Shippers (your customers): Manufacturers, distributors, retailers, construction companies, agricultural businesses, and e-commerce companies—each with unique shipping needs
  • Carriers (your service providers): Owner-operators, small/medium/large fleets, asset-based and non-asset carriers with different capabilities and service levels
  • Intermediaries: Freight brokerages, 3PLs, freight forwarders, and load boards connecting ecosystem participants
  • Regulators: FMCSA (primary), DOT (parent agency), state agencies, and industry associations setting standards

How Money Flows

  • The chain: Customer pays you → You pay carrier → Margin is your gross profit
  • Your earnings: Commission split with brokerage (typically 50/50)
  • Key insight: Your income grows with volume AND margin—both matter

Market Dynamics

  • Tight capacity (more loads than trucks) = higher rates, easier to find freight, harder to find trucks
  • Loose capacity (more trucks than loads) = lower rates, harder to find freight, easier to find trucks
  • Seasonal patterns: Pre-holidays, harvest seasons, and month/quarter-end create tight capacity; winter is typically slower
  • External factors: Economic conditions, weather events, regulatory changes, and fuel prices all affect rates

Your Competition

  • Direct competitors: Other agents, large brokerages, 3PLs, and digital freight platforms
  • Indirect competitors: Asset-based carriers with brokerage arms, direct shipper-carrier relationships
  • Your advantage: Personal service, specialized knowledge, and relationship-based trust

Specialization Paths

  • Geographic: Specific lanes, regions, or rural/hard-to-serve areas
  • Industry: Food & beverage, construction, manufacturing, retail
  • Equipment: Flatbed, reefer, tanker, heavy haul, specialized
  • Service level: Expedited, white-glove, cross-border, high-value
★ Remember
Understanding the industry landscape helps you identify opportunities others miss. When capacity tightens, shippers need reliable agents more than ever. When capacity loosens, your carrier relationships become your competitive edge. Success comes from reading the market and adapting your approach accordingly.
