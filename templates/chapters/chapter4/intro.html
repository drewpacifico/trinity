Chapter 4

Truck Types and Specifications

Equipment knowledge is the technical foundation of freight brokerage. You can't effectively match freight to equipment, quote loads accurately, or communicate credibly with carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. without understanding what each trailer type can handle.

In this chapter, you'll learn:

  • Dry van trailers: The industry workhorse—dimensions, capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates., and when to use them
  • Refrigerated trailers (reefersRefrigerated Trailer (Reefer)Insulated trailers with refrigeration units maintaining temperatures from -20°F to +70°F. Essential for produce, dairy, meat, and temperature-sensitive goods.): Temperature control, product requirements, and operating considerations
  • Flatbed trailers: Open-deck hauling, securing requirements, and tarping
  • Specialized flatbedFlatbed TrailerAn open platform trailer (48' or 53' long) without sides or roof, used for freight requiring top/side loading or exceeding enclosed trailer dimensions. variants: Step decksStep Deck (Drop Deck) TrailerA flatbed with two deck levels (higher front section, lower rear section at ~3.5 feet off ground), accommodating 10-11 foot tall freight., lowboysDouble Drop (Lowboy) TrailerA flatbed with three deck levels including a very low center section (18-24 inches off ground), allowing 12-15+ foot tall freight., RGNsRGN (Removable Gooseneck) TrailerA lowboy trailer where the front section detaches to create a loading ramp, allowing wheeled equipment to roll on and off., and Conestogas
  • Power only operations: When carriers provide tractors for customer-owned trailers
  • Specialized equipment: TankersTanker TrailerTrailers hauling liquids (fuel, chemicals, food products) with 5,000-9,000 gallon capacity, heavily regulated especially for hazmat and food-grade., hoppersHopper TrailerTrailers that haul dry bulk materials like grain, plastic pellets, or cement, unloading through bottom gates or pneumatic systems., livestock trailersLivestock TrailerSpecialized multi-deck trailers with ventilation for transporting live animals, subject to strict animal welfare regulations., and auto carriers
  • Truck classifications: Weight ratings and what they mean for your business
  • Axle configurations: Weight distribution and legal requirements
  • Equipment matching: A systematic process for selecting the right equipment
  • Carrier communication: How to clearly specify equipment needs
★ Key Point
Equipment knowledge separates professional agents from amateurs. You'll use this information every day—sometimes dozens of times per day. Master this material now and you'll quote loads confidently, make fewer costly mistakes, and build credibility with carriers and customers alike.
