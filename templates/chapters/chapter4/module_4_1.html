Introduction to Equipment Knowledge

Understanding truck and trailer types is fundamental to your success as a freight agent. You can't effectively match freight to equipment if you don't know what each trailer type can handle. You can't quote loads accurately if you don't understand equipment limitations. You can't communicate credibly with carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. if you use incorrect terminology or make unrealistic equipment requests.

This chapter provides comprehensive information about the most common equipment types you'll encounter. The knowledge you gain here will be used every single day of your career.

Why Equipment Knowledge Matters

  • Accurate quoting: Know what equipment a load requires before you price it
  • Effective carrier communication: Speak the language carriers understand
  • Problem prevention: Avoid booking wrong equipment that can't handle the freight
  • Professional credibility: Demonstrate expertise that builds trust with customers and carriers
  • Cost optimization: Select appropriate equipment without overspending on unnecessary features
★ Key Takeaway
Study this material thoroughly. Equipment knowledge separates professional agents from amateurs who waste everyone's time by quoting inappropriate equipment or making impossible requests.

Practical application

Questions to ask yourself as you learn equipment types:

  • "What equipment type does this freight require?" Practice this mental exercise with every load you encounter.
  • "Why can't I use a different equipment type?" Understanding limitations reinforces your knowledge.
  • "What would happen if I sent the wrong equipment?" Visualize consequences to cement the importance of accuracy.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Dry vans first: They handle most general freight.
