Dry Vans

  • Dimensions: 53' × 8.5' × 9' interior, ~43-45K lbs capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates., 4,000 cu ft

  • Key concept: Cubing out (filling space before weight) vs. weighing out (reaching weight before filling space)
  • Requirements: Dock-height loading or liftgateLiftgateHydraulic platform attached to trailers that raises and lowers freight at locations lacking loading docks, with 3,000-5,000 pound capacity limits. service

Flatbeds

  • Dimensions: 48' or 53' × 8.5' wide, deck height ~5' off ground
  • Capacity: 45-48K lbs
  • Height limit: 13.5-14' total (load + deck) without permits
  • Securing: Chains, straps, blocking, bracing required
  • Tarping: $50-150 additional when needed
  • Best for: Steel, lumber, machinery, construction materials, oversized items

Specialized FlatbedFlatbed TrailerAn open platform trailer (48' or 53' long) without sides or roof, used for freight requiring top/side loading or exceeding enclosed trailer dimensions. Variants

  • Step Deck: Lower deck ~3.5' off ground, carries 10-11' tall freight
  • Double Drop (LowboyDouble Drop (Lowboy) TrailerA flatbed with three deck levels including a very low center section (18-24 inches off ground), allowing 12-15+ foot tall freight.): 18-24" well section, carries 12-15'+ tall freight, 50K+ lbs capacity
  • RGN: Detachable front creates ramp for wheeled equipment
  • Conestoga: Rolling tarp system, $100-300 premium over standard flatbed

Weight Regulations

  • Federal maximum: 80,000 lbs gross
  • Axle limits: 12K steer, 34K drive axles, 34K trailer axles
  • Key point: Distribution matters—not just total weight

Equipment Selection Process (8 Steps)

  1. Check physical dimensions (length, width, height, door openings)
  2. Verify weight capacity
  3. Determine temperature control needs
  4. Confirm loading/unloading method
  5. Assess weather protection requirements
  6. Check special regulations (hazmatHazmat (Hazardous Materials)Regulated substances posing risks to health, safety, property, or environment, classified into nine DOT classes and requiring special handling., food-grade, permits)
  7. Consider customer preferences
  8. Select economically appropriate equipment

Common Equipment Selection Mistakes

  • Quoting dry van for oversized freight
  • Quoting reefer when temperature control unnecessary
  • Forgetting liftgate requirements
  • Not accounting for all weight components (pallets, packaging)
  • Assuming all trailers have same capacity
  • Missing permit requirements for oversizeOversize/Overweight (OS/OW)Freight exceeding legal dimensions (8.5' wide, 13.5-14' tall, 53' long) or weight (80,000 lbs), requiring special permits and possibly escorts. loads
★ Remember
Equipment knowledge is foundational to your success. You'll use this information every day—sometimes dozens of times per day. The difference between a professional agent and an amateur is often knowing which equipment type a load actually requires.
