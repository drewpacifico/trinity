Chapter 3

Summary Sheet

Six Core Responsibility Categories

  • Sales and Business Development: Prospecting, qualifying, presenting, closing, and relationship management
  • Load Booking and Coordination: Gathering details, determining equipment, pricing, finding carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners., confirming shipments
  • Operational Execution: Tracking, problem resolution, documentation, and customer communication
  • Financial Management: Invoicing, carrier payment, margin management, credit management, dispute resolution
  • Administrative and Compliance: Carrier vetting, record keeping, regulatory compliance, technology management
  • Continuous Learning: Market intelligence, skill development, relationship building

Sales Process Flow

  • Prospecting: Identify potential customers through research, networking, and observation
  • Qualifying: Determine if prospects are worth pursuing based on volume, fit, and payment history
  • Presenting: Communicate your value by focusing on their specific needs and challenges
  • Closing: Convert prospects with low-risk trial opportunities
  • Relationship Management: Grow accounts through consistent value and communication

Load Booking Flow

  • Step 1: Gather complete details (locations, dates, commodity, weight, special requirements)
  • Step 2: Determine equipment requirements based on freight characteristics
  • Step 3: Price the load considering market conditions, difficulty, and relationship
  • Step 4: Find a qualified carrier at a rate that preserves margin
  • Step 5: Confirm everything in writing with rate confirmation

Time Management Priority Order

  • First: Active shipments and customer service (always takes priority)
  • Second: Sales and prospecting (fill the gaps between operational needs)
  • Third: Administrative tasks (batch daily for efficiency)
  • Fourth: Learning and development (build into routine continuously)

Success Mindset Principles

  • Ownership: Every load is your responsibility regardless of whose fault problems are
  • Long-term thinking: Relationships over transactions, reputation over short-term profits
  • Proactive communication: Inform before being asked, address issues before they escalate
  • Continuous improvement: Learn from every experience, seek feedback, refine processes
  • Resilience: Handle setbacks without losing motivation or professionalism
  • Ethical standards: Integrity builds reputation; reputation drives long-term success
