Module 3.8

Time Management and Prioritization

With so many responsibilities competing for your attention, effective time management becomes critical. How you allocate your limited hours determines your success.

Customer Service Comes First

Active shipments (freight currently in transit or scheduled for imminent pickup) always take priority. When a customer calls with an urgent issue or a carrierCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. needs direction, drop everything else and handle it immediately.

Why? Because active shipments represent commitments you've already made. Failing to manage them properly damages relationships with both customers and carriers. A delayed delivery costs your customer money. A carrier who can't reach you when they need guidance may never accept your loads again.

⚠ Important
Schedule your day around the reality that operational issues can arise anytime and require immediate attention.

Sales Fills the Gaps

When active shipments are running smoothly—freight has been picked up, is tracking on time, and no issues are anticipated—shift focus to business development.

Morning and mid-afternoon are typically best times for prospecting calls. Decision-makers are more available then compared to late afternoon when they're wrapping up their day.

★ Key Takeaway
Remain flexible. If an operational issue arises during your scheduled prospecting time, handle the operational issue. You can make prospecting calls later, but you can't recover from a service failure.

Administrative Tasks Daily

Set aside time each day for essential administrative work:

  • Morning review: Check active shipments, review emails, respond to overnight messages, identify priorities for the day
  • Invoicing: Bill completed deliveries daily rather than letting invoices accumulate
  • Documentation: Save PODsProof of Delivery (POD)Signed documentation confirming freight was delivered, required for invoicing customers and proving shipment completion., update files, and organize paperwork while details are fresh
  • Carrier payment processing: Review and approve carrier invoices requiring payment
  • Financial tracking: Update your margin tracking and accounts receivable monitoring
💡 Pro Tip
Batching administrative tasks—doing all invoicing at once, processing all carrier payments together—improves efficiency compared to switching between admin and operations repeatedly throughout the day.

Learning Happens Continuously

Don't wait for perfectly available time to focus on learning—it won't happen. Instead, build learning into your daily routine:

  • Morning routine: Spend 15-30 minutes reading industry news while drinking your coffee
  • Commute time: Listen to freight industry podcasts or sales training audio
  • Slow periods: When waiting for callbacks or during brief gaps, read articles or review training materials
  • Weekly review: Dedicate an hour each week to analyzing your performance and studying relevant topics

Continuous small investments in learning compound over time into significant expertise.

Typical Daily Routine

While every day varies based on operational demands, a general structure helps maintain productivity:

Morning (7:00-9:00 AM)

  • Check all active shipments and confirm pickups scheduled for today
  • Review emails and voicemails, responding to urgent matters
  • Update customers on any overnight developments
  • Quick scan of industry news and market updates

Mid-Morning (9:00-11:00 AM)

  • Prospecting calls to new prospects or follow-up with existing leads
  • Quote requests from customers
  • Carrier outreach for loads needing coverage
  • Handle administrative tasks requiring focus

Midday (11:00 AM-1:00 PM)

  • Check active shipments, call drivers if needed
  • Book new loads as opportunities arise
  • Respond to customer inquiries
  • Lunch while staying available for urgent calls

Afternoon (1:00-4:00 PM)

  • More prospecting or customer relationship calls
  • Quote additional loads
  • Find carriers for newly booked freight
  • Process paperwork and invoicing for completed deliveries

Late Afternoon (4:00-6:00 PM)

  • Final check on all active shipments
  • Confirm tomorrow's pickups with carriers
  • Send end-of-day updates to customers as needed
  • Organize documentation and plan tomorrow's priorities
★ Key Takeaway
This structure provides framework while remaining flexible enough to handle the unpredictable nature of freight operations. Respond immediately to operational issues as they arise throughout the day.

Practical application

Questions to ask yourself about your time management:

  • "What are my active loads right now?" You should know instantly, without looking anything up.
  • "Did I make my prospecting calls today?" If operational work always crowds out sales, your pipeline will dry up.
  • "What administrative tasks am I behind on?" Invoices not sent and documents not filed create future problems.
  • "When did I last learn something new?" Continuous improvement requires actual time investment.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Operations first, always: Never let a shipment problem wait while you do something else. Your reputation depends on execution.
  • Protect prospecting time: Block time for sales calls and treat it as seriously as customer appointments.
  • End-of-day organization: Spend 15 minutes daily organizing files, updating notes, and planning tomorrow.

Perfect time management isn't the goal. The goal is consistently prioritizing the right activities even when the day doesn't go as planned.
