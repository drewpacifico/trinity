Chapter 3

The Role of a Freight Agent

Now that you understand the industry landscape, it's time to dive deep into what you'll actually be doing every day. This chapter breaks down the complete role of a freight agent into manageable components.

In this chapter, you'll learn:

  • Your core responsibilities: The six major categories of work that define your daily activities
  • Sales and business development: How to prospect, qualify, present, close, and manage customer relationships
  • Load booking and coordination: The step-by-step process from gathering details to confirming shipments
  • Operational execution: Tracking, problem resolution, documentation, and customer communication
  • Financial management: Invoicing, carrierCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. payment, margin management, and dispute resolution
  • Administrative and compliance: Carrier vetting, record keeping, and regulatory requirements
  • Continuous learning: Market intelligence, skill development, and relationship building
  • Time management: How to prioritize and structure your day for maximum effectiveness
  • The success mindset: Mental frameworks that separate thriving agents from struggling ones
★ Key Point
As a freight agent, you're running a business within a business. Every day requires you to wear multiple hats. Understanding these different roles and how they interconnect helps you allocate your time effectively and develop the skills that matter most.
