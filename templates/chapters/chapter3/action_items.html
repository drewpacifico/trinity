Chapter 3

Action Items

Complete these tasks to establish effective work habits and systems:

  • Map out your typical daily schedule: Create a template showing when you'll focus on each responsibility category, building in flexibility for operational issues
  • Assess your strengths and weaknesses: Honestly evaluate your current abilities across the six responsibility categories. Which areas need the most development?
  • Block prospecting time in your calendar: Schedule specific times for business development activities and protect that time unless operational emergencies arise
  • Set up a shipment tracking system: Whether using your brokerage's TMSTMS (Transportation Management System)Software combining freight operations with customer relationship management, often provided by brokerages to agents., a spreadsheet, or another tool, establish a system that shows all active loads at a glance
  • Create a load information checklist: Build a form ensuring you collect all essential details before quoting any load—complete addresses, dates, commodity, weight, dimensions, special requirements
  • Establish your carrierCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. vetting process: Confirm your brokerage's carrier qualification requirements and create your personal checklist for using new carriers
  • Set up a documentation filing system: Organize digital files so you can locate any document for any shipment within 60 seconds
  • Write your personal professional standards: Document your commitments regarding response times, communication quality, and service standards. Post where you'll see it daily
  • Create a daily checklist: List the non-negotiable tasks you must complete every day—checking active loads, invoicing deliveries, following up on quotes
  • Schedule weekly business review time: Block one hour weekly to review your performance metrics, margin analysis, and improvement opportunities
