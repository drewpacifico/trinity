Chapter 8

Action Items

Complete these tasks to master sales strategies:

  • Develop your consultative discovery questions: Write 10-15 open-ended questions you'll ask every prospect to understand needs, challenges, priorities, and decision process
  • Create value quantification examples for your services: Calculate time savings, prevented problem costs, inventory optimization, etc. in dollar terms prospects can easily understand
  • Write 3-5 customer success stories: Use the situation-problem-solution-results structure. Choose stories representing different customer types and challenges you solve
  • Practice LAVA method for common objections: Role-play with colleague or mentor: rate too high, need to think, burned before, need approval, only use carriers
  • Prepare negotiation parameters for typical lanes: For your most common lanes, establish: target rate, walk-away rate, market rate range, trade elements available
  • Schedule regular customer check-ins: Set recurring calendar reminders for Tier A (weekly), Tier B (bi-weekly), Tier C (monthly) customer touchpoints
  • Identify upsell/cross-sell opportunities in current customer base: Review existing customers: Who ships additional lanes you don't handle? Who uses only one equipment type but needs others?
  • Create relationship-building calendar: Schedule quarterly account reviews with major customers, plan for holiday acknowledgments, set reminders for customer milestones
  • Build market intelligence sharing system: Subscribe to capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates. reports, rate indexes, industry news. Create schedule for sharing relevant insights with customers monthly
  • Develop your personal sales metrics: Track: contact-to-conversation ratio, conversation-to-quote ratio, quote-to-close ratio, average deal size
  • Practice your 30-second credibility statement: Write and rehearse until natural: who you are, your specialization, one impressive credential
  • Create call-to-action templates: Write 5-7 low-risk CTAs for different situations: trial shipment, backup lane, overflow freight, pilot program
