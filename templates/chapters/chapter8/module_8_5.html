Module 8.5

Handling Objections Professionally

Objections aren't rejections—they're requests for more information or expressions of concern that need addressing. Welcome objections as opportunities to clarify misunderstandings and resolve concerns.

The LAVA Method for Objection Handling

LAVA provides a systematic approach to addressing objections effectively:

L - Listen

Allow customers to fully express their concern without interrupting. Show you're listening through verbal acknowledgments ("I understand," "I see") and body language. Don't interrupt to defend yourself.

A - Acknowledge

Validate their concern as legitimate rather than dismissing it:

  • "I understand why that's important to you."
  • "That's a legitimate concern."
  • "I appreciate you raising that."

V - Verify

Ensure you understand the real objection rather than assuming:

  • "Just to make sure I understand, your main concern is [restate objection]—is that correct?"
  • "Is there anything else about [topic] that concerns you?"

A - Answer

Address the concern directly with specific information, examples, or alternatives. Your answer should specifically address their verified concern.

Common Objections and Effective Responses

"Your rate is too high."

Verify: "When you say too high, are you comparing to a specific quote, or do you have a target rate in mind?"

Answer: "Let me explain what's included: verified insurance, proactive tracking, 24/7 problem resolution. One prevented problem pays for the rate difference on 20 loads."

"I need to think about it."

Verify: "Is there specific information you need, or would you like time to evaluate other options?"

Answer: Based on their response, either provide needed information or schedule specific follow-up.

"We've been burned by brokersBrokerAn individual or company licensed by the FMCSA to arrange transportation of freight between shippers and carriers without owning trucks. Acknowledge: "I'm sorry you had that experience. I understand why you'd be cautious."

Verify: "What specifically went wrong? I want to address your concerns directly."

Answer: Explain how you're different and offer a trial shipment to prove yourself.

"We only work directly with carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners., not brokers."

Verify: "Have you experienced situations where your preferred carriers didn't have capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates.?"

Answer: "That's exactly where we add value—not replacing your carrier relationships, but supplementing them when they're unavailable."

💡 Pro Tip
Sometimes what people say initially isn't their real concern. Verification uncovers the actual issue. Ask follow-up questions before jumping to answer what you think they're concerned about.

Practical application

Practice the LAVA method with common objections:

  • "Your rate is too high" - Verify what they're comparing to, then explain total value.
  • "I need to think about it" - Verify if they need more information or time, then provide what's needed.
  • "We've been burned by brokers before" - Acknowledge their experience, verify what went wrong, then explain how you're different.
  • "We only work with carriers directly" - Verify if they've had capacity issues, then position yourself as backup.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Listen fully: Don't interrupt or get defensive. Let them express the full concern.
  • Verify before answering: What they say initially may not be the real concern.
  • Welcome objections: They indicate interest. Uninterested prospects don't bother objecting.

Objections are opportunities to provide information and build trust. Handle them professionally and you'll convert more prospects.
