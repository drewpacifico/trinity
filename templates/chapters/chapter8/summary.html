Chapter 8

Summary Sheet

Consultative Selling Process

  • Discovery (80% listening): Ask open-ended questions to understand needs, challenges, priorities
  • Analysis: Identify patterns, recognize problems you can solve, match services to priorities
  • Recommendation: Restate situation, explain specific solutions, provide concrete examples
  • Validation: Confirm fit, invite questions, engage prospects in evaluation

Value vs. Price

  • Price-based selling: Creates unsustainable business (thin margins, wrong customers, no differentiation)
  • Value-based selling: Focuses on total cost and total benefit, not just upfront rate
  • Quantify value: Time savings, reduced delays, inventory optimization, customer retention, risk mitigation

Customer Buying Motivations

  • Cost-driven: Need competitive pricing, show value even while discussing price
  • Relationship-driven: Value trust and partnership, emphasize personal accountability
  • Quality-driven: Want best solutions, highlight vetting process and performance metrics
  • Convenience-driven: Need simplicity and time savings, emphasize how you simplify
  • Innovation-driven: Seek advanced solutions, showcase technology and continuous improvement

Sales Presentation Structure

  • Establish credibility (30 sec): Brief intro with relevant credential
  • Demonstrate understanding (1-2 min): Summarize their situation
  • Present solutions (2-3 min): Explain how you address specific needs
  • Address concerns (1-2 min): Anticipate objections proactively
  • Call to action (30 sec): Specific, low-risk next step

LAVA Objection Handling

  • Listen: Allow full expression without interrupting
  • Acknowledge: Validate concern as legitimate
  • Verify: Ensure understanding of real objection
  • Answer: Address specifically with facts, examples, or alternatives

Storytelling Structure

  • Situation: Similar customer
  • Problem: Specific challenges they faced
  • Solution: How you helped
  • Results: Quantified improvements

Negotiation Tactics

  • Prepare thoroughly: Know your numbers, understand their position, identify leverage
  • Never make first offer when possible
  • Anchor high but reasonably
  • Trade concessions, don't cave to price requests
  • Use silence strategically
  • Find creative solutions: Volume discounts, flexible timing, payment terms
  • Know when to walk away from unprofitable business

Relationship Building

  • Regular communication: Appropriate to customer tier (weekly/monthly/quarterly)
  • Provide market intelligence and insights
  • Proactive problem-solving before issues arise
  • Ask for feedback and act on it
  • Build personal connections beyond business
  • Conduct account reviews quarterly/annually

Upselling/Cross-selling

  • Upselling: Additional lanes, increased frequency, premium services, volume commitments
  • Cross-selling: Additional equipment types, different service levels, other divisions/locations
  • Only suggest genuinely beneficial services
  • Time appropriately (after proving value)
  • Frame as opportunities (not pressure)
★ Remember
The fundamental shift in effective selling is from "How can I sell my services?" to "How can I help solve their logistics challenges?" When you genuinely focus on helping customers succeed, sales become a natural outcome rather than a forced transaction.
