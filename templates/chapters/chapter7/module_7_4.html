Module 7.4

Initial Contact Strategies

First impressions matter enormously. Your initial contact should be professional, respectful of prospects' time, and focused on their needs rather than your desire to sell.

Cold Calling Fundamentals

Despite digital communication growth, phone calls remain highly effective for B2B prospecting when done professionally.

Best Times to Call

Tuesday through Thursday, 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM generally produce best results. Avoid Monday mornings (people catching up), Friday afternoons (mentally checked out), and lunch hours.

Getting Past Gatekeepers

Receptionists and administrative assistants control access to decision-makers. Treat them respectfully—they often influence who gets through.

  • Be direct and professional: "I'm calling to speak with whoever handles freight shipping. Can you point me in the right direction?"
  • If asked "What's this regarding?": "It's regarding transportation services for your facility"
  • Don't try to trick gatekeepers—be honest about initial outreach
  • Build relationships with gatekeepers over time; they can become allies

Opening Statement (15-20 Seconds)

Your opening should be concise, professional, and immediately convey why you're calling:

"Good morning, my name is [Your Name] with [Brokerage]. I work with [industry type] companies managing their trucking needs. I noticed [specific observation about their business]. I'm calling to see if you'd be open to a brief conversation about how we might help with your shipping operations."

Qualifying Questions

Once someone is willing to talk, ask open-ended questions:

  • "Tell me about your shipping operations—what does a typical week look like?"
  • "What types of products do you ship most frequently?"
  • "What are your primary shipping lanes?"
  • "How do you currently arrange your trucking?"
  • "What's most important to you in freight service—price, reliability, speed?"
  • "What challenges have you experienced with freight shipping?"
★ Key Takeaway
The conversation should be 80% them talking, 20% you talking. You're gathering information, building rapport, and identifying whether they're a qualified prospect worth pursuing. Listen carefully and ask follow-up questions.

Email Outreach

Subject Lines That Work

  • "Trucking Support for [Company Name]"
  • "Question About Your [Specific Product] Shipments"
  • "Backup Capacity for [Specific Lane]"

Email Structure

Keep initial emails brief (150-250 words maximum):

  • Paragraph 1: Brief introduction and why you're contacting them specifically
  • Paragraph 2: Value proposition relevant to their situation
  • Paragraph 3: Soft call to action

Follow-Up Sequence

  • Day 3-4: Brief check-in
  • Day 10-14: Share relevant content
  • Day 21-28: Different angle on value proposition
  • Day 45-60: Final attempt before longer-term nurture
💡 Pro Tip
Make each follow-up provide value rather than simply asking "Did you see my previous email?" Share market updates, industry articles, or relevant insights that demonstrate your expertise.

Practical application

Questions to practice for initial contact:

  • "Tell me about your shipping operations." Open-ended questions get prospects talking.
  • "What types of products do you ship most frequently?" Understand their freight mix.
  • "What challenges have you experienced with freight shipping?" Identify pain points you can address.
  • "Would you be open to receiving a quote on an upcoming shipment?" Soft close to move forward.

Where to focus

For new agents, prioritize:

  • Listen more than you talk: Aim for 80% listening, 20% talking during initial conversations.
  • Handle objections gracefully: "We already have providers" isn't rejection. It's an opportunity to offer backup capacity.
  • Follow up consistently: Most sales happen after 5-12 touches. Persistence pays.

Initial contact is about starting relationships, not closing deals. Focus on learning about prospects and demonstrating professionalism.
