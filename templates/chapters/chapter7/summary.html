Chapter 7

Summary Sheet

Ideal Customer Characteristics

  • Regular shipping needs: 1-2+ loads monthly minimum
  • Appropriate freight types: Matching your expertise and carrierCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners. network
  • Growth potential: Volumes will increase over time
  • Reasonable expectations: Understand logistics realities
  • Timely payment: History of paying vendors according to terms

Customer Types

  • Manufacturers: Predictable schedules, established relationships, value reliability, price-sensitive
  • Distributors: High volume, multiple destinations, speed-focused, very price-competitive
  • Retailers: Regular inbound, strict receiving requirements, appointment-sensitive
  • Construction: Specialized equipment, premium rates, irregular patterns, project-driven
  • E-commerce: Fast growth, lack logistics expertise, unrealistic expectations possible
  • Agricultural: Extreme seasonality, rural locations, weather-dependent, relationship-based

Prospecting Methods

  • Online: Company websites (careers, locations, products), business directories (ReferenceUSA, Hoovers), LinkedIn (decision-makers, company pages), business journals, industry publications
  • Geographic: Industrial parks, port areas, distribution corridors, manufacturing zones
  • Referrals: Existing customers, carriers, industry peers, business service providers

Initial Contact

  • Cold calling: Best times Tuesday-Thursday 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM, respect gatekeepers, 15-20 second opening, qualifying questions (80% listening, 20% talking)
  • Email: Specific subject lines, brief structure (150-250 words), relevant value proposition, soft call to action, follow-up sequence
  • In-person: Local small/mid-sized companies, professional appearance, respectful of time, leave materials, follow up within 48 hours

Building Credibility

  • Demonstrate knowledge: Through intelligent questions and relevant insights
  • Set realistic expectations: Honest about challenges, acknowledge limitations
  • Start small: Request trial shipments, backup lanes, overflow freight
  • Provide value beyond price: Market intelligence, routing suggestions, process improvements
  • Professional presentation: Prompt responses, clear quotes, proper follow-through

Common Objections and Responses

  • "Already have providers" → Position as backup/overflow capacity
  • "Send information" → Use as opportunity to ask qualifying questions
  • "Happy with current providers" → Ask about times when providers were unavailable
  • "No time now" → Schedule specific callback time
★ Remember
Building a customer base takes time and persistent effort. Focus on quality prospects who match your capabilities, demonstrate value through expertise and service, and build relationships that grow over time. Underpromise and overdeliver to build lasting trust.
