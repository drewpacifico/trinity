Chapter 7

Action Items

Complete these tasks to build an effective prospecting system:

  • Create list of 50 target prospects in your area: Use ReferenceUSA, LinkedIn, and geographic prospecting to identify specific companies with names, addresses, and approximate size
  • Set up CRMCRM (Customer Relationship Management)A database tracking all prospect and customer information, interactions, and scheduled activities. It's the foundation of systematic follow-up. or tracking system for prospects: Whether sophisticated software or simple spreadsheet, create system tracking: company name, contact information, interactions, next follow-up date, notes
  • Develop your 15-20 second introduction: Write and practice your opening statement until it sounds natural and confident. Record yourself and listen critically.
  • Write email templates for different prospect types: Create 3-5 templates for manufacturers, distributors, retailers, construction, etc. Personalize each before sending but have structure ready.
  • Practice handling common objections: Role-play with colleague or mentor responding to "we already have providers," "send information," "too busy," etc.
  • Identify 3-5 potential referral sources: List existing customers, carriersCarrierA company or individual that transports goods from one place to another, including asset-based carriers, owner-operators, and fleet owners., or business service providers who might refer prospects. Reach out and explain how they can help.
  • Schedule daily prospecting time blocks: Reserve specific times (ideally 9-11 AM Tuesday-Thursday) exclusively for prospecting calls. Protect this time from operational interruptions.
  • Create qualifying question checklist: Write 8-10 questions you'll ask every prospect to determine shipping volume, freight types, lanes, current providers, decision process, pain points
  • Research major employers in your region: Identify top 20-30 manufacturers, distributors, or companies in your target industries within 50 miles. These should be your highest-priority prospects.
  • Join relevant LinkedIn groups: Find and join 3-5 groups for your target industries (construction, manufacturing, food/beverage, etc.). Engage authentically, don't just spam sales messages.
  • Drive through 2-3 industrial areas: Spend a few hours visiting industrial parks, noting company names, photographing buildings with visible signage and loading docks for later research
  • Prepare referral request script: Write specific language you'll use to ask satisfied customers for referrals: "Do you know anyone at [company] who might benefit from our services?"
  • Set up follow-up sequences: Create templates for Day 1 (thank you), Day 3-4 (value email), Day 7-10 (call), Day 14-21 (value), etc. Build these into your CRM or calendar system.
  • Create value-add content library: Compile 5-10 items you can share with prospects: market updates, industry articles, seasonal capacityCapacityThe availability of trucks relative to loads needing transport; tight capacity (more loads than trucks) increases rates, loose capacity (more trucks than loads) decreases rates. forecasts, rate trend analyses, logistics tips
  • Establish weekly prospecting goals: Set specific targets: X new prospects identified, Y cold calls made, Z emails sent, A conversations completed. Track and measure weekly.
